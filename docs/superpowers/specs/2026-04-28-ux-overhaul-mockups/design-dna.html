Design DNA from your references

Airbnb · Apple · Stripe — the threads they share

1. Confident display typography
Headline
Hero text is unmissable. Not the 14px system header MarkFlow uses today.
2. Generous whitespace
Air around every element. Apple is the extreme; Stripe is ample; Airbnb still breathes.
3. Two-button CTA
Universal across all three references — pill-shaped, filled + outline.
4. Imagery / artwork as anchor
Photography (Airbnb), product (Apple), organic gradient (Stripe). Every hero has visual weight.
5. Clean horizontal nav
Logo  ·  Convert  ·  Bulk  ·  Search  ·  Settings
Top bar only. No sidebar. Thin, confident, dropdowns where needed.
6. Disciplined accent color
One brand color does all the work. Airbnb red, Apple blue, Stripe purple.

The pivotal question: how literally?

Your references are marketing/consumer surfaces. MarkFlow has dense ops surfaces (bulk tables, batch lists, log streams, 30+ pages). How far should we lean?

A

Premium feel — reduce density to match

Fewer screens. Fewer items per screen. Tables become richer cards. Settings become a guided flow, not a 15-section page. If something doesn't fit at this density, it hides behind progressive disclosure or moves to an "advanced" view. The app feels like Apple did the ops tool.

B

Hybrid — marketing chrome, dense ops content

Header, nav, landing/Convert page, brand color, typography all match the references. Inside the dense pages (Batch Management, Bulk Jobs, Logs), data tables and lists stay roughly as dense as today but with the new type, spacing, and color polish. Most pages still exist; the chrome is what unifies them.

C

Polish only — same structure, premium veneer

All 30+ pages stay. IA stays. Just apply the design tokens — typography scale, spacing scale, button shapes, accent color, soft shadows — over the top of what exists. Lowest risk; least transformation.
